# Voice & content

Plano's voice is **confident, quiet, and editorial** — written by a thoughtful architecture magazine, not
a consumer social app. Get the words wrong and the typography can't save it.

---

## Tone

- **Sentence case for everything** except small all-caps tracked labels (`TRACK`, `COLLECT`,
  `EXPLORE NEARBY →`) and CTAs. Titles, buttons, headings — sentence case. The uppercase labels are an
  architectural convention, not shouting.
- **Second person, sparingly.** Copy frequently drops the pronoun and lets the verb carry the action:
  "Track visits, rate buildings, and follow friends." — not "You can track visits…".
- **Commands over promises.** "Log your journey." "Curate lists." "Follow architects." Imperative, direct,
  no hedging.
- **No hype words.** Never "amazing," "incredible," "revolutionary," "delightful," "unleash." Trust the
  typography and photography to carry the drama.
- **Typographic detail.** Real em dashes (—), proper apostrophes (the world's), `·` middots for inline
  separators (`user · 12 buildings`).
- **No emoji. Anywhere.** Not in copy, headings, or empty states. Typographic Unicode (— · →) is fine;
  decorative emoji break the register.
- British or American spelling — the codebase uses both (`catalogue` and `cataloged`). No need to unify.

## Product copy

- **Tagline:** "The world's architecture, cataloged."
- **Sub:** "Track visits, rate buildings, and follow friends."
- **Section eyebrows** in the feed are tiny uppercase labels above massive building names:
  `FEATURED`, `NEARBY YOU`, `NEW IN LONDON`.

## The rating language (Michelin, 1–3)

| Dots | Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Impressive** | Worth a detour |
| 2 | **Essential** | Worth a journey |
| 3 | **Masterpiece** | Once in a lifetime |

- They are **awards, not stars** — most buildings have none, and that's complete.
- A rating ≥ 2 triggers the one playful line in the whole product:
  **"You just boosted this building's rank!"** Everything else stays quiet.

## Eyebrow → headline pairings (the editorial rhythm)

| Eyebrow | Headline |
|---|---|
| `VISITED` | Barbican Centre |
| `WANTS TO VISIT` | Unité d'Habitation |
| `COLLECTION · 12 BUILDINGS` | Brutalist Gems of London |
| `TOP 1% · BUILT 1931` | Villa Savoye |
| `ARCHITECT` | Le Corbusier |

## Microcopy patterns

- **CTAs (editorial):** uppercase tracked + arrow — `REVIEW THIS BUILDING →`, `EXPLORE NEARBY →`,
  `READ MORE →`, `DIRECTIONS →`.
- **Buttons:** sentence case — "Log visit", "Save to list", "Follow", "Open the feed".
- **Empty states:** one imperative line — "Log your first visit to start your catalogue." No apology, no
  cartoon.
- **Meta lines:** `who · action · when` with middots; counts in monospace (`12 buildings · 4 collections`).
- **Actions/verbs in the feed:** "Visited", "Wants to visit", "Published a collection · 12 buildings".

## Words to avoid

Hype adjectives (amazing/incredible/revolutionary/delightful), exclamation marks (except the one rating
toast), ALL-CAPS sentences, emoji, "click here", and anything that sounds like a growth-marketing email.
